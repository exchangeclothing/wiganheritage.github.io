Minutes of Encompass 'task & finish' meeting held at The Grange 09/07/2009

Present: Carol Watson (Chair Hon Member), Pam Stewart (Hon Member), Clare Brennan & Elaine Jones (Over 50's), Khaled Amini, (BME) Joe Taylor (Heritage), Phil Hurst BWCN

This meeting was a response to a decision at the previous meeting (16.06.09) regarding the following agenda item:
What constitutes a 'community network' and which organisations are therefore eligible to nominate members to Encompass.

It was agreed that Encompass is a strategic body that aims to act in a transparent, open and professional way, being accountable to both members and partner organisations. Therefore it is appropriate that a criterion for membership is developed for Encompass to adequately fulfil the role required as a full partner within the LSP framework.

It was agreed that transparency and clarity of purpose were important and that a network should embrace the entire borough. It was pointed out that any organisation, group or individual can attend ASCEND meetings and gain the experience required at strategic level. Should two of more constituted networks be formed, relating to the same issue, it would be Encompass's role to facilitate and enable the groups to reach consensus and develop their own organisations by electing members from each to create a network umbrella group that would serve the needs of all.

Khaled sought clarification regarding his position within Encompass as the BME representative to the LSP. The constitution sates that elected community representatives to Wigan Borough Partnership & Thematic Partnerships may attend and contribute to Encompass meetings but will not vote.

It was agreed that for a network to be eligible to have representation on Encompass:
The governance document (constitution) should be visible online, reflect that the organisation is indeed a network, open to groups and individuals with similar interests, non-political and non-discriminatory (this will inform members of the public what the network sets out to do).
The minutes of meetings should be visible online to demonstrate that the network is accountable by holding regular meetings attended by representative member groups within the network (this will inform members of the public how the network conducts its business, the decision making processes and who attends meetings).

Two representatives to Encompass should be elected from each network at an AGM (or an EGM called specifically for that purpose) and the term should be for two years. It was suggested that one representative be elected for one year and the other for two years. This would create a rolling system that will encourage mentoring and skills development for members of Encompass plus ensure both representatives do not leave office at the same time.

The elected representatives would agree to a role description, (that will be provided by Encompass, and must be discussed with the representative before they agree to put themselves forward). The role description will make it clear that Encompass is the strategic group and only strategic matters relevant to all networks will be discussed at meetings.

It was suggested that applications for membership should be tabled at the Ascend Group meetings, this will give people an opportunity to support groups and give encouragement where appropriate, plus strengthen the links, but agreement was that, as the strategic and answerable body, Encompass has the final say as to whether or not a network seeking membership fulfils the criterion.

Reference to the discussion at the previous meeting regarding newly formed networks eligibility to Encompass was noted and reflected upon. New members will have an opportunity to join Encompass as either a co-opted group or developing group but without voting rights.

Reference was made to the way that other organisations co-opt inexperienced members to the board in a non-voting capacity to gain capacity and to be supported and mentored by more experienced members until such time as they gain the capacity to become full members.

It was agreed that a full membership and a co-opted membership system would give the degree of flexibility needed. Decisions on the entry level of membership would be Encompass's responsibility. New membership applications would be treated as an agenda item and decisions would be evidence based.

That being the only agenda item, the meeting closed.
