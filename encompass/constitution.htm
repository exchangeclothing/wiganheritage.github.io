Encompass

Constitution

Name:

The group will be called Encompass.

Mission Statement:

To oversee and direct activities within the Wigan Borough relating to community empowerment and to develop relationships, procedures and events that maximise community involvement

Encompass shall work at a strategic level within the borough to represent, promote and support the 3rd Sector. Within the Encompass structure, operational delivery shall be carried out by ASCEND (Association for Social Community and Environmental Network Development).

Aims

To act as the umbrella body that facilitates community participation in the Wigan Borough Partnership, the Thematic Partnerships and such other Council appointed groups and organisations that request it to do so.

To ensure that the aspirations of the people of the borough are reflected in the aims and objectives of the Wigan Borough Partnership, though the maintenance of strong and effective channels of communication.

Encompass shall recognise and embrace Wigan Borough's diverse community. Encompass shall recognise that the strength of the Borough lies in its local people and Encompass shall therefore be committed to making the most of what we have locally; encouraging people to feel part of where they live and to contribute to the everyday life and well being of the Borough.


4. Objectives

1. Support the involvement of residents (especially those from excluded and seldom heard groups) in decision-making, especially in deprived areas
2. Enable the voice of local communities and priority community interests to be heard and reflected in the strategic and locality partnerships. Strengthen community cohesion, engagement and capacity
3. Support the delivery of better public service outcomes in priority neighbourhoods
4. To regularly bring together individuals from voluntary and community organisations to discuss issues, consultation processes and decisions on local service delivery
5. To provide information about issues, consultation processes and decisions and, guidance on representing the views of members/users
6. To provide opportunities for individuals from voluntary and community organisations to engage with local issues
7. To provide opportunities for local partners to consult and involve local people
8. To increase the capacity of local people to get involved
9. To encourage participation by informing local people on the impacts of their involvement.

Area of Operation

Encompass will operate within the Borough of Wigan.

Powers

To further the aims and objectives of Encompass, the organisation shall have the following powers:
a) To raise funds and apply for, invite, obtain, collect and receive contributions from any other person or persons, charitable trusts, statutory bodies or companies whatsoever by way of subscription, donation, grant, legacy and otherwise.
b) To borrow through loans, mortgages or other means for the organisation's objectives.
c) To employ, on such terms and conditions of employment that Encompass shall determine, any paid or voluntary worker or workers to assist in the attainment of the said aims and objectives.
d) To do all such other lawful things as shall further the attainment of the said aims and objectives.
e) To open and operate any such bank accounts or other accounts as the management of Encompass deems necessary

Responsible Body:

If necessary, Encompass shall appoint a Responsible Body to manage any staff required to service the Community Networks and be free to review that appointment at any time.

Communication Policy:

A communication policy will be accepted to ensure that Encompass members are in regular contact with each other, meet on a regular basis, understand that they are playing a significant role in an important community organisation and are immediately informed when anything concerning Community Empowerment occurs throughout the Borough. The method of communication must be user friendly and available to everyone.

Membership

Encompass will endeavour to ensure that the Community Networks represent all segments of the community across the Wigan Borough, including the 'hard-to-reach' groups.

Membership will consist of a maximum of two representatives from each of the current community networks, any future community networks and the honorary officers of ASCEND.

Elected community representatives to Wigan Borough Partnership & Thematic Partnerships may attend and contribute to Encompass meetings but will not vote. If consensus cannot be reached, the vote will be one vote per Network with the Chair having the casting vote.

Membership can be reviewed when necessary.

The following bodies will be co-opted members, with no voting rights:
Wigan & Leigh CVS, Township Team, Community Engagement Team, Douglas Valley Community Partnership. Officers from these bodies will be invited to meetings as appropriate.

It is the responsibility of each affiliated Network to ensure that it is represented at Encompass meetings by at least one of its two representatives and that feedback is received from the nominated/chosen representatives.

Substitutes:

A designated Network substitute can attend meetings in exceptional circumstances


Activities of Encompass:

Encompass will be administered by those community representatives which are nominated by or elected from the Community Networks, until as such time as Encompass shall call an Annual General Meeting and thereupon after at any subsequent Annual General Meeting.

Encompass will have the power to co-opt additional representatives from community organisations or groups. Co-opted members of Encompass must provide a written mandate to represent their organisation or group.

Encompass shall set in place, on behalf of the Community Networks, a process for the selection of representatives to the Local Strategic Partnership Board, the Strategic Management Group, the Thematic Partnerships, the LAA Working Group, the Third Sector Liaison Group and any other group that requires community representation within the Wigan Borough.

There will be no maximum number of Community Networks eligible for representation on Encompass. If less than three Community Networks provide representatives, Encompass will review its continued existence.

ASCEND will offer opportunity for participation by individuals acting in a non-representative capacity.

Honorary Officers:

Encompass shall appoint a Chair, a Vice Chair, a Treasurer and a Secretary to help manage its affairs.

The Duties of the Honorary Officers

a) The duties of the Chairperson shall be:
1. To preside at meetings of the organisation.
2. As may be required by the organisation, to act as the principal spokesperson on public occasions or when representations are being made on behalf of the organisation to public bodies.

b) The duties of the Vice Chairperson shall be:
1. To take over the role of Chairperson whenever the Chairperson is unable to do so.

c) The duties of the Secretary shall be:
1. To prepare in consultation with the Chairperson the agenda for the meetings of the committee
2. To take and keep minutes of all meetings.
3. To collect and disseminate information on all matters affecting the organisation.

d) The duties of the Treasurer shall be:
1. To supervise the financial affairs of the organisation.
2. To ensure that proper accounts are kept with regards to all monies received by and paid out by the organisation.

The Honorary Officers of Encompass shall meet as often as deemed necessary and appropriate, in addition to attending full membership meetings.

Code of Conduct

All members of ENCOMPASS shall agree to conduct themselves at any meeting they attend in compliance with the procedure at general meetings as set out below.

General Meetings of Encompass

Meetings will be held each month, unless otherwise deemed appropriate. At least one meeting per year will be dedicated to planning and reflection. The quorum at meetings will be at least 4 voting members.

Minutes shall be kept in a minute book retained by the Secretary and copies circulated to all members prior to meetings.

The Chair at all times, when present, shall occupy the Chair, but if still absent five minutes after the time appointed to start, and without prior notification, the Vice Chair or any other member of Encompass elected by those present shall take the Chair. The powers of the Chair shall be invested in any Chairperson of the meeting for the duration of that meeting.

All remarks must be addressed to the Chair.

The Chair shall have the discretion to limit the length of discussion on any subject and to impose a time limit on speakers. All remarks shall be confined strictly to the matter that is under discussion.

Only one person shall be allowed to speak at a time and the Chair shall decide whom shall be allowed to speak and when.
If the Chair rises to call a member or the meeting to order or in order to direct the course of the proceedings of the meeting, any member who is speaking shall cease to do so.
If after repeated rulings by the Chair, a member refuses to obey, that member may be ordered
to leave the meeting.

In the event of a vote of no confidence being proposed in the Chair or any other Encompass member, this must be seconded and if carried by a two-thirds majority of those present and voting, the member concerned shall cease to carry out his or her duties for the remainder of that meeting only.

Membership to Encompass can be revoked for non-attendance and inappropriate behaviour by a two-thirds majority of those present and voting at a full membership meeting. Should that occur, that member's Network would be requested to nominate a replacement
Decisions are to be reached on the basis of consensus whenever possible. Minority views will be sympathetically considered but, following full discussion, will not prevail over the majority.

In the case of an equality of votes the Chairperson shall have a second or casting vote

Encompass will have the power to set up and schedule ad-hoc groups as needed. It will also have the power to invite other groups/organisations/individuals to all or any meetings, as required. These groups will not have voting rights but will be able to bring information and request resources from Encompass.

Finance

a) Any money obtained by the Encompass shall be used only to forward the aims of Encompass.
b) Any bank accounts opened for the Encompass shall be in the name of Encompass.
c) Any cheques issued shall be signed by the Treasurer and at least one other nominated official(s) who are not related.
d) The Treasurer will be responsible for maintaining proper records and financial systems.

Special General Meetings:

A Special General Meeting may be called by any four Encompass Members to discuss any urgent matter. The Secretary shall give all members a minimum of ten days notice of any Special General Meeting together with notice of the business to be discussed.

The quorum for any Special General Meeting shall be four.


Annual General Meeting:

ENCOMPASS will arrange an Annual General Meeting (hereinafter referred to as the AGM) each May, or as close to that month as is considered practicable.

The members of all Community Networks with representatives on Encompass shall be invited to attend the AGM and will have voting rights at the AGM.

Prior notice of motions to be included on the agenda should be given seven days before the AGM, to facilitate the setting of the agenda but motions will also be taken from the floor, debated and voted upon. Decisions taken at the AGM are binding.

The quorum for the AGM shall be ten.

The business of the AGM shall include:

· Receiving a report from the Chairperson on the activities of Encompass over the year
· Receiving a report from the Treasurer on the finances of Encompass
· Electing members to Encompass for the following year, following democratic procedures
· Business relating to the activities of Encompass will be openly discussed.
· Considering any other matters as may be decided.


Equal Opportunities:

Encompass shall operate in a non-discriminatory way. Encompass will avoid discriminating on the grounds of age, race, gender, disability, sexuality, religion or class.

Amendments:

Amendments to this Constitution may be proposed at any meeting of Encompass, but no vote will take place on its adoption until the following meeting. During this time all voting members will be sent details of the proposed amendment and informed that a vote will take place at the next meeting of Encompass, or at a Special General Meeting.

No amendment can be made unless 2/3 of those voting members attending the next meeting after an amendment is proposed agree to the amendment.

No alterations can be made to the dissolution clause below.

Dissolution

A proposal to dissolve Encompass can be made at any meeting of Encompass, but no vote will take place until the next meeting of Encompass is called. All voting members of Encompass will be sent details of the proposal to dissolve Encompass and be informed that a vote will take place at the next meeting of Encompass.

Encompass will only be dissolved if 2/3 of the voting members attending the next meeting, after Encompass proposes the dissolution do so agree. The Responsible Body will then immediately pay all outstanding debts and return the remaining Encompass funds to the source from which those funds originated or, where possible, to agree with the funding body a plan for the disbursement of unspent funds to the Encompass member Community Networks.
