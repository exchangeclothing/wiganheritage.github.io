How do we deal with the proposed interim LSP funding:

A proposition that the LSP provide £20,000 to Encompass to cover ongoing running costs for the Community Networks until the end of this financial year was put to Encompass by Simon dale and Ann Roberts, Policy Officer - Partnership/Voluntary Sector Policy and Planning Team, on Friday 3rd October, with terms and conditions attached. This proposition was discussed at length.

Clare Brennan pointed out that the eligible costs outlined mean that Over 50's would be able to receive virtually no support. They have been faced with a 400% increase in officer rent and unless alternative office space is secured their network will struggle to survive, however, she was not advocating for a change in the criteria to accommodate the Over 50's.

It was pointed out that the stipulation that training costs are only acceptable if CVS don't provide the necessary courses was detrimental. Instances of CVS charging community groups for inadequate training were sited. CVS courses that provide 'attendance certificates' rather than an accreditation are not considered to be optimum. A general dissatisfaction with CVS training was expressed.

Pam Stewart suggested that we clarify if any under spend will have to be returned at the end of six months and suggested we contact the Participatory Budgeting Unit for further advise.

Action: Ed Ellis will contact the Participatory Budgeting Unit and report back.
